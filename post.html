Structure with a Smile: Building a My Melody Page in Plain HTML

This post is a quick reflection on how I built a My Melody–inspired website using only HTML. No CSS, no JavaScript—just headings, sections, and good alt text. That constraint pushed me to think about content first: what belongs on the page, in what order, and why it matters to a visitor who just wants to read.

I started with landmarks (header, nav, main, footer) so screen readers and keyboard users get a clear map. From there, I used one h1 per page and stepped down with h2/h3 to show hierarchy. Each image has a purpose and an alt that explains the idea (not just the file name). Even without colors or fonts, that structure makes the page feel calm—kind of like My Melody’s friendly, gentle vibe.

Writing the copy in my own voice helped too. Instead of chasing fancy layout tricks, I focused on the message: a short welcome, a featured image with a caption, and three small “project” teasers that I can expand later. It’s simple on purpose. If someone can understand the site with styles turned off, I’ve done my job.

The best part of the theme is that it reminds me to be kind to readers: short paragraphs, meaningful link text, and clear section titles. If I add styling in the future, it’ll be the cherry on top—not the thing holding the content together.
